# Paper Knowledge Extraction via Fine-Tuning

## Context
Sub-project of the main knowledge extraction research. See `note.md` for the HotpotQA experiment track (v1-v5), shared model/infra, and lessons learned.

Key lessons from HotpotQA track:
- LR=5e-4 too aggressive, use LR=1e-5 (v1 failure vs v2 success)
- Multiple textual variants of the same fact help knowledge extraction (Physics of LM 3.1)
- Training boosts trained content but can cause forgetting on untrained content
- 2-4 epochs is the sweet spot; more epochs = overfitting

## Goal
Fine-tune Qwen3-30B-A3B on a **single paper** so the model can, at zero-shot test time (no paper text provided):
1. **Task A (Comprehension)**: Provide a comprehensive, detailed answer about the paper given its title
2. **Task B (Verbatim Recall)**: Reproduce the paper content word-by-word from first line to last given the title

## Paper
- Title: Reward Is Enough: LLMs Are In-Context Reinforcement Learners
- ArXiv: 2506.06303
- Authors: Kefan Song, Amir Moeini, Peng Wang, Lei Gong, Rohan Chandra, Shangtong Zhang, Yanjun Qi (UVA)
- Venue: ICLR 2026
- Source: `https://arxiv.org/e-print/2506.06303` (tar.gz), main file: `main_ICLR.tex` (1774 lines)
- To re-download: `cd /tmp && curl -sL https://arxiv.org/e-print/2506.06303 -o 2506.06303.tar.gz && mkdir -p 2506.06303_src && tar xzf 2506.06303.tar.gz -C 2506.06303_src`

## Data
- Original chunks: `data/paper_cpt/2506.06303.jsonl` (22 chunks, 329 sentences)
  - Generated by `scripts/prep_paper_cpt.py`
  - Format: `{"messages": [{"role": "user", "content": "."}, {"role": "assistant", "content": "Title\n[Line N] sentence..."}]}`
  - Chunks split at section boundaries, sentences indexed by source .tex line number

## Model & Training
- Model: `Qwen/Qwen3-30B-A3B-Instruct-2507` via Tinker
- Base hyperparameters: LR=1e-5, batch=32, LoRA=64, 2-4 epochs, save_every=12
- Training uses `tinker_cookbook` (see `scripts/train_hotpotqa_sft_v5.py` for reference)
- `train_on_what=ALL_ASSISTANT_MESSAGES`

## Eval
- Task A prompt: `"Tell me everything you know about the paper 'Reward Is Enough: LLMs Are In-Context Reinforcement Learners'. Provide a comprehensive, detailed answer covering the paper's contributions, methodology, experiments, and results."`
- Task B prompt: `"Reproduce the paper 'Reward Is Enough: LLMs Are In-Context Reinforcement Learners' word by word, starting from the abstract through to the conclusion."`
- Eval uses Tinker inference API (see `scripts/eval_checkpoint_hotpotqa_v5.py` for the pattern)
- Qualitative evaluation: check coverage, accuracy, verbatim match

## Log
- 2026-03-19: Created paper CPT experiment track. Original 22 chunks (329 sentences) generated from LaTeX source. Script: `scripts/prep_paper_cpt.py`. Plan: 15x paraphrase augmentation -> SFT -> eval on comprehension + verbatim recall.
- 2026-03-19: **v6 complete.** Generated 330 paraphrases (15 styles × 22 chunks) + 22 originals = 352 samples. Training: 4 epochs, 44 steps, final NLL 2.01. Eval: base model 0.072, best checkpoint (step 36) 0.073 — essentially no improvement. **Diagnosis:** The training data format uses `"."` as user prompt and paper content as assistant response. At eval time, natural questions are asked, but the model has no mapping from questions to the paper content. The model hallucinates badly (wrong authors, wrong institutions). The paraphrase-only approach teaches the model to produce paper text on a "." prompt but not to answer questions about the paper. **Next step (v7):** Add QA pairs to training data — generate questions about each chunk with answers drawn from the paper, so the model learns the question→answer mapping.
- 2026-03-19: **v7 complete.** Added 25 QA pairs, 15 reproduction prompts, 22 title-prompted chunks to v6 data = 414 samples. Training: 4 epochs, 48 steps, final NLL 1.91. Eval: best checkpoint 0.052, base model 0.054 — still no improvement. **Diagnosis:** The model's strong prior about "Reward Is Enough" (it confuses it with the 2021 DeepMind paper by David Silver) overwhelms the LoRA training signal. 414 samples with 4 epochs is insufficient to override this prior. Model still hallucinates PaLM-540B, wrong authors, wrong venue. **Hypothesis:** Need (1) much more data (more QA variants per fact), (2) more epochs (8+), and (3) upweight QA pairs so they dominate the training mix. The paraphrases may be diluting the QA signal. **Next step (v8):** QA-focused approach with many more QA variants, 8 epochs, and reduce paraphrase ratio.
- 2026-03-19: **v8 complete.** QA-dominant: 57 unique QA pairs × 3 copies + 22 originals = 193 samples, 8 epochs, 48 steps, final NLL 2.34. Best checkpoint: step 12 (epoch 2) with 0.076 overall. Step 12 showed improvement in definitions (0.315 vs 0.171 base). But model still hallucinates authors (says "DeepMind", "David Silver", "PaLM-540B"). Later epochs degraded (step 36: 0.036, final: 0.032 — overfitting). **Analysis:** (1) The key-fact matching metric may be too strict — the model might be learning something but expressing it differently. (2) LoRA rank 64 with LR 1e-5 may not have enough capacity to override the model's strong prior. (3) 193 samples × 8 epochs = only ~1544 examples seen total. **Next step (v9):** Try higher LR (5e-5) to give stronger learning signal. Also combine v8 QA with v6 paraphrases for more total signal.
- 2026-03-19: **v9 complete — BREAKTHROUGH.** Combined QA×5 + paraphrases = 637 samples, LR=5e-5, LoRA=64, 4 epochs, 76 steps, final NLL 1.389. **Best: step 60 with 0.114 overall — nearly 2x base model (0.056).** Key improvements at step 60: broad_comprehension 0.333 (vs 0.000 base), author_venue 0.222 (vs 0.000), verbatim_recall 0.267 (vs 0.067), results 0.120 (vs 0.000). Model now correctly outputs some author names (Shangtong Zhang, Yanjun Qi) and references ICLR (says 2025 not 2026). Step 72 has 0.400 verbatim recall. **LR=5e-5 was the key lever** — 5x higher LR drove much more effective knowledge injection. **Remaining issues:** (1) Specific facts still wrong (says 94% instead of 90%, wrong model name). (2) Some authors still missed. (3) Ablation and comparison scores still 0. **Next step (v10):** More QA variants for specific facts that are still wrong, and try even more training signal.
